# Phishing Attack Detection Using Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a major technology in modern cybersecurity, especially in the detection of phishing attacks. Phishing is a cyberattack technique in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, financial details, or personal information by pretending to be legitimate organizations. Traditional phishing detection systems mainly depend on blacklists and predefined rules, which are often ineffective against newly created phishing emails and websites. AI-based systems improve phishing detection by analyzing patterns and automatically identifying suspicious behavior.

Machine Learning algorithms are commonly used in phishing detection and classification systems. Supervised learning techniques such as Decision Trees, Random Forests, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and Neural Networks are trained using datasets containing both legitimate and phishing URLs, emails, and website characteristics. These algorithms examine features such as URL length, domain age, spelling mistakes, email headers, embedded links, and webpage structure to determine whether a website or message is malicious.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) also plays an important role in phishing detection systems. NLP methods enable AI models to analyze the writing style and language used in suspicious emails and messages. Fraudulent emails often contain urgent wording, grammatical mistakes, fake branding, or emotional manipulation designed to trick users into sharing confidential information. AI systems can identify these linguistic patterns and classify phishing messages with high accuracy.

Data preprocessing is an important stage in phishing detection because cybersecurity datasets often contain noisy, redundant, or inconsistent data. Techniques such as feature extraction, tokenization, normalization, and dimensionality reduction help improve model performance and reduce false-positive rates. Public datasets from sources such as PhishTank and Kaggle are commonly used for training and evaluation in phishing detection research.

Despite its advantages, AI-based phishing detection still faces several challenges. Cybercriminals constantly modify phishing techniques to bypass intelligent detection systems. Adversarial attacks, AI-generated phishing content, and encrypted communication channels can reduce model accuracy. In addition, maintaining real-time phishing detection requires strong computational resources and frequent model updates.

In conclusion, Artificial Intelligence has significantly enhanced phishing detection systems by enabling automated analysis and intelligent threat identification. Its ability to analyze large communication datasets, recognize malicious patterns, and detect phishing attacks in real time makes it an essential technology in modern cybersecurity defense systems.
